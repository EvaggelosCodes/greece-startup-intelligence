## TIER 1: DEMAND SIGNALS (what people actually want and pay for)

### Job Boards — what companies are hiring = what's growing
- https://www.kariera.gr — Greece's largest job board, search by category
- https://www.skywalker.gr — second largest, strong in tech
- https://gr.linkedin.com/jobs — filter to Greece, sort by date
- https://www.xo.gr — classifieds including jobs
- Search: site:kariera.gr "[category]" to find open roles + salaries
- Search: "hiring Greece" + [category] on LinkedIn
- Signal: A spike in job postings for a role type = that sector is scaling = a gap to fill as a service

### Google Trends — what Greeks are actively searching
- https://trends.google.com/trends/?geo=GR — filter to Greece
- Compare categories: which searches are growing vs declining?
- Look for searches with high volume but few quality results = opportunity
- Search trends for: "πώς να", "τι να κάνω", "καλύτερο", "ψάχνω" (how to, what to do, best, looking for)
- Rising searches = emerging demand

### App Store & Play Store — what exists, what's missing, what's broken
- Search Greek App Store (change region to Greece): top free, top paid by category
- Search Google Play store filtered to Greece
- Look at reviews of existing Greek apps — complaints = product gaps
- Categories to check: food, finance, real estate, services, education, health, travel
- A category with no Greek app or only bad-reviewed apps = opportunity

### Skroutz.gr — the Greek Amazon, real price and demand data
- https://www.skroutz.gr — search any product category
- Check review counts = actual purchase volume
- Check "popular" sections = what Greeks are actually buying
- Look at price ranges — what do Greeks pay for X?
- Categories with many sellers but bad reviews = quality gap
- Categories with few sellers but high search = supply gap

### E-food.gr and Wolt Greece — food/delivery demand
- https://www.e-food.gr — check which restaurant categories are most popular
- Check which areas are underserved (islands, suburbs, second cities)
- Look at restaurant review counts and average ratings

### Airbnb/Booking.com — tourism demand signals
- Search Airbnb in Greek locations — what's fully booked? What price?
- Check Booking.com reviews for what tourists complain about
- Look at "experiences" on Airbnb Greece — what's popular, what's missing
- Search Tripadvisor Greece reviews — what do tourists want that doesn't exist?

## TIER 3: SOCIAL INTELLIGENCE (what real people say, unfiltered)

- https://www.reddit.com/r/Athens/

- Search Reddit: "Greece" + "recommend" / "looking for" / "best" / "worst" / "disappointed"
- Search Reddit: "Greece" + business type to find real experiences

### Greek Facebook Groups (where real Greeks talk about money and business)
- Search Facebook: "επιχειρήσεις Ελλάδα" (businesses Greece)
- Search Facebook: "ελεύθεροι επαγγελματίες" (freelancers)
- Search Facebook: "digital nomads Greece"
- Search Facebook: "εκπαίδευση ενήλικων Ελλάδα" (adult education Greece)
- Search Facebook: "κατοικίδια Ελλάδα" (pets Greece)
- Search Facebook: "ομαδαγορά" type groups (group buying)
- Note: You can't log into Facebook, but you can search for group activity in public posts

### Greek Twitter/X — business and startup community
- Search: "startup ελλάδα" on Twitter/X
- Search: "επιχείρηση 2026" on Twitter/X
- Search: "#Greecestartup" "#startupgreece"
- Search: Greek entrepreneurs with large followings for what they're discussing

### Greek YouTube — what content gets views = what people want to learn
- Search YouTube: "επιχείρηση ελλάδα" (business Greece)
- Search YouTube: "πώς να βγάλω λεφτά ελλάδα" (how to make money Greece)
- Search YouTube: "freelancer ελλάδα"
- View counts and comment sections reveal real demand and pain points

### TikTok Greece — fastest signal for youth market trends
- Search TikTok: #ελλάδα #επιχείρηση
- Look at what Greek financial/business creators are posting about
- Comments = real market feedback from young Greeks

### Trustpilot & Google Reviews — competitor weakness = your opening
- Search Trustpilot for Greek companies in any category you're researching
- Read 1-star reviews — they are a direct brief for what to build better
- Search Google Maps for any business type in Greece — read reviews critically
- Bad reviews of existing players = your product spec

## TIER 4: COMPETITOR DEEP INTELLIGENCE

### Greek Business Search
- https://www.businessregistry.gr — official Greek business registry (GEMI)
- Search GEMI: find how many businesses in a category, when they were founded
- https://www.taxisnet.gr — note: can reveal business registration trends
- https://opencorporates.com — filter to Greece for company data
- Search: "[business type] Αθήνα τιμοκατάλογος" (Athens price list) to find pricing

### App Store Intelligence
- Search "[category] Greece" in App Store reviews to find complaints
- Look at update frequency of competitor apps — dead apps = abandoned market
- Check download estimates via sensor tower (free tier) for Greek apps

### LinkedIn Company Intelligence
- Search LinkedIn companies: Location=Greece, Industry=[target], Founded=2020+
- Look at headcount changes — growing companies validate the market
- Look at employee skill sets — reveals what the company actually does

### Price Discovery (critical — most research skips this)
- Search skroutz.gr for any physical product to get real Greek price ranges
- Search Google: "[service] τιμή ελλάδα" (price Greece) for service pricing
- Search Airbnb Experiences Greece — what do people charge for experiences?
- Look at fiverr.com filtered to Greek sellers — what do they charge?
- Check workathlon.gr for freelance rates in Greece

## TIER 5: DIASPORA & INTERNATIONAL SIGNALS

### Greek Diaspora (3-4 million Greeks abroad)
- Search: "Greek products abroad" "Greek food export" "Greek diaspora market"
- Reddit: r/greek — Greek diaspora community
- Facebook: Greek diaspora groups in USA, Australia, Germany, UK
- What do diaspora Greeks buy online that they can't get locally?
- What do they want from Greece that nobody ships them?

### International Signals Arriving in Greece
- What trend just hit Germany/France/UK that will reach Greece in 12-18 months?
- Search: "trend EU 2025 2026" + filter for categories without Greek players
- ProductHunt: search for products that could work in Greece but have no Greek competitor
- Search: "Greece" on ProductHunt to see what's been built already

### Digital Nomad Intelligence (high-spend demographic)
- NomadList.com — search Greece cities for nomad activity
- Nomad-specific subreddits and Facebook groups
- What do nomads complain about in Greece vs other destinations?
- What services do they pay for elsewhere that don't exist in Greece?

## TIER 6: TIMING & REGULATORY SIGNALS

### Upcoming Windows
- Check Greece national holidays — what economic activity spikes?
- Easter in Greece = massive domestic tourism + food spending
- Summer season (June-September) = tourism peak, island economy
- September/October = back to school, business planning season
- November-February = slow season = opportunity for off-season services

### Regulatory Pipeline
- European Parliament legislation tracker — what's coming to Greece via EU?
- Search: "νόμος 2026 ελλάδα" (law 2026 Greece) for upcoming regulations
- Check ΕΦΚΑ (EFKA) website for freelancer/self-employment regulation changes
- Ministry of Labor website for gig economy regulation updates
- Short-term rental regulation — check current status (changed in 2024-2025)
- AI Act implementation in Greece — what does it require, who needs to comply?

## HOW TO USE THIS FILE EACH RUN

Don't try to check everything every day. Instead:

1. **Always check (every run):** Tier 1 demand signals for your top 3 research gaps
2. **Rotate through:** Tier 2 sources — pick 3-4 different ones each day
3. **Weekly deep-dive:** Tier 3 social intelligence (Reddit + reviews)
4. **When evaluating a specific idea:** Run full Tier 4 competitor check
5. **Once a week:** Tier 5 diaspora + Tier 6 timing signals

The goal is to hit sources that no other analyst would think to check.
The signal is in the places that aren't obvious.

# Mike's Search Playbook - how I learn to hunt better
*Read before searching and update after. The point is that run 50 searches better than run 1.*

---

## What Works
- Greek-first for laws, prices and complaints; English only for nomad, tourist, diaspora, or app-store/global competitor checks.
- Add the year plus law/deadline words when researching regulation.
- `site:taxheaven.gr` is high-signal for tax/e-invoicing timing.
- Search official deadline source + pricing + social truth for compliance ideas. Example: `site:taxheaven.gr ilektroniki timologisi 2026`, then `myDATA timi`, then reddit/social complaint search.
- For marketplaces, search app names plus local intent. App visibility is a competitor signal even before local market share is proven.

## What Wastes Tokens
- Vague English "Greece business opportunity" searches.
- Fetching pages when snippets already answer timing, price or competitor existence.
- Broad competitor-landscape searches before one exact "already exists?" query.
- Treating a free government compliance tool as irrelevant. It changes the business model.

## Source Signal Ratings
- taxheaven.gr / official tax snippets - HIGH for regulation and timing.
- Greek vendor pricing pages - MED/HIGH for price discovery, but biased toward paid solutions.
- Reddit/social snippets - HIGH for pain language, LOW for market size.
- App stores / global marketplace pages - MED for competitor existence, LOW for Greek supply density unless local listings are visible.

# Research Methodology
*HOW to research, not just where. This is what separates analyst-grade research from journalism.*

---

## THE CORE PRINCIPLE: Triangulate Everything

Never act on a single source. Every claim needs 3 independent signals before it becomes a STABLE_FACT.
One source = HYPOTHESIS. Two sources = TRACKED_SIGNAL. Three+ sources = STABLE_FACT.

---

## METHOD 1: Demand Archaeology
*Find where demand is hiding before it becomes obvious*

1. Search Google Trends (Greece filter) for a category — is the trend UP or DOWN?
2. Search kariera.gr for jobs in that category — are companies hiring? What roles?
3. Search skroutz.gr for that category — how many products, what review volume?
4. Search Reddit r/greece + r/digitalnomad for the category — what do people complain about?
5. Search Google: "[category] Greece problems" / "[category] Ελλάδα προβλήματα"

If steps 1-3 show growing signals AND step 4-5 show real complaints = validated demand.

---

## METHOD 2: Competitor Autopsy
*Read the graveyard to understand the living*

1. Find 2-3 existing players in the space (use greek-competitor-hunter skill)
2. Read their 1-star reviews on Google Maps, Trustpilot, App Store
3. Check their last update/post — are they active or abandoned?
4. Check their LinkedIn — are they hiring or shrinking?
5. Check their pricing — is it higher than the market will bear?

The gap = what customers keep complaining about that the competitor never fixes.

---

## METHOD 3: Job Board Signal Extraction
*Job postings are the most honest market signal that exists*

1. Search kariera.gr for "[skill/role]" — note total results and date of postings
2. Repeat on skywalker.gr and LinkedIn Greece
3. Search for the SAME role a year ago (Google: site:kariera.gr "[role]" before:2025-01-01)
4. Is the number growing? = that sector is scaling
5. What skills are listed in the job description? = what tools/services are in demand

A company hiring for X means X is valuable enough to pay full-time salary for.
That same X delivered as a service to multiple companies = a business.

---

## METHOD 4: Price Discovery Research
*Know what Greeks actually pay before building a pricing model*

1. Search skroutz.gr for the product — what's the price range?
2. Search Google: "[service] τιμή Αθήνα" and "[service] κόστος ελλάδα"
3. Check Workathlon.gr for freelance service rates
4. Check Airbnb Experiences Greece for experience pricing
5. Check Facebook Marketplace Greece for informal pricing
6. Find 3 existing providers and note their prices

Now you know: floor price (cheapest competitor), ceiling price (premium), and where the gap is.

---

## METHOD 5: Regulatory Window Hunting
*EU funding + law changes = the best startup timing signals in Greece*

1. Check espa.gr "Ανοιχτές Προσκλήσεις" (open calls) — is there money available?
2. Search: "ΕΣΠΑ" + [category] + current year
3. Check European Parliament tracker for laws in pipeline that will hit Greece
4. Search: "νόμος" + [category] + "2026" on Google News
5. Note the gap between law announcement and enforcement = window to build

The best Greek startup timing: build AFTER a law is announced but BEFORE it's enforced.
Companies need compliance solutions before they're legally required to have them.

---

## METHOD 6: Social Listening Synthesis
*Aggregate what real Greeks say about a category*

1. Reddit r/greece — search the category keyword, sort by Top/Year
2. Google: "forum ελλάδα" + [category] + "προβλήμα" (problem)
3. Google: "[category]" + "site:reddit.com" + "Greece"
4. TripAdvisor reviews for tourist-adjacent categories
5. Google Maps reviews for physical-service categories

Copy the exact language of complaints. This is your marketing copy.
"I couldn't find a reliable X in Greece" = your tagline.

---

## METHOD 7: Diaspora Gap Analysis
*3-4M Greeks abroad who want something from Greece*

1. Search Greek diaspora Facebook groups for product requests
2. Search: "Greek [product/food/service] abroad" — what's hard to find?
3. Search r/greek (diaspora subreddit) for pain points
4. Search Greek-American, Greek-Australian community websites
5. Check what Greek products are sold on Amazon internationally — gap if not found

Diaspora market = premium pricing + dollar/euro income + high emotional motivation to buy Greek.

---

## METHOD 8: The "What If It Already Exists" Test
*Before getting excited about an idea — verify it's not already solved*

Run this EVERY TIME before adding an idea to the pool:

1. Google: "[idea] ελλάδα" AND "[idea] Greece"
2. Skroutz.gr: search the product/service
3. App Store Greece + Google Play: search the category
4. LinkedIn: companies in Greece in this exact space
5. Crunchbase: filter to Greece, search the category
6. Google Maps: search the service in Athens, Thessaloniki

Only if this search returns: nothing, or only bad players, does the idea survive.

---

## QUALITY STANDARDS FOR EACH CLAIM

| Claim Type | Minimum Evidence Required |
|------------|--------------------------|
| "There is demand for X in Greece" | Google Trends up + 2 Reddit complaints + job postings |
| "No competitor exists" | Checked all 6 sources in Method 8 |
| "Greeks will pay €Y for X" | Found 3 price data points from Method 4 |
| "This is the right timing" | Law/funding announcement OR 3 converging signals |
| "Score is X.X/10" | Each sub-score justified with 1 specific source |

---

## WHAT LAZY RESEARCH LOOKS LIKE (avoid these)

- "There is growing demand for X in Greece" — with no source
- "No strong competitor exists" — without checking all 6 sources in Method 8
- "Greeks are increasingly using X" — based on one news article
- Scoring an idea 8/10 on market size with no TAM calculation
- Generating an idea on day 3 that you could have generated on day 1 without new research
- Using the same 5 sources every single day

# Research Methodology — Cheat Sheet
*Analyst-grade research, minimum tokens. Read once; it rarely changes.*

---

## THE ONE RULE: Triangulate from snippets
- 1 source = HYPOTHESIS. 2 = TRACKED_SIGNAL. 3+ = STABLE_FACT.
- Get all 3 from **search snippets**, not page fetches. Three agreeing snippets = done.
- Fetch a page only when a specific number you need is in none of the snippets.

---

## THE 6 MOVES (use the one that fits the question)

1. **Demand check** — Is it growing? `[category] google trends Greece` + `site:kariera.gr [role]` (jobs = real budget) + `site:skroutz.gr [product]` (price + volume). Growing trend + hiring + complaints = validated.

2. **Competitor autopsy** — `[competitor] reviews Greece`. Read the 1-star snippets. What they keep complaining about = the gap = your product spec. Copy their exact words for marketing.

3. **Price discovery** — `[service] τιμή ελλάδα` + check skroutz range. Floor (cheapest), ceiling (premium), gap = your price. Never score MARKET without ≥1 real price point.

4. **Regulatory window** — `ΕΣΠΑ [category] [year]` (open money) + `νόμος [category] 2026`. Best timing = after a law is announced, before it's enforced (compliance demand window).

5. **Social truth** — `[category] site:reddit.com/r/greece`. "I can't find a reliable X in Greece" = your tagline. Real complaints > theory.

6. **"Already exists?" test** — before any idea enters the pool: `[idea] ελλάδα` + skroutz + Google Maps in one search pass. Survives only if nothing or only bad players show up.

---

## QUALITY BAR (each claim needs this, nothing more)
| Claim | Minimum evidence |
|-------|------------------|
| "Demand exists" | trend up + 1 complaint + jobs signal (1-2 searches) |
| "No real competitor" | 1 "already exists?" search came up empty/weak |
| "Greeks pay €Y" | 1 real price point (more is better) |
| "Right timing now" | a law/funding signal OR 3 converging snippets |
| "Score is X.X" | each sub-score tied to 1 specific snippet |

## LAZY RESEARCH = banned
- Any claim with no source. "Growing demand" from one article. Fetching a page when a snippet would do. Generating an idea you could've had yesterday with no new search.
